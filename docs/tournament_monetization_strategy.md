# Tournament Platform Monetization Strategy & Roadmap

## Overview

This document outlines a **practical monetization and growth roadmap**
for a tournament platform while the product is still being developed
part‑time. The strategy prioritizes **adoption first**, then introduces
monetization gradually once real usage appears.

The goal is to avoid friction early while still designing the system so
monetization can be introduced easily later.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Industry Monetization Models

Major tournament platforms such as:

-   Start.gg
-   Challonge
-   Battlefy

typically rely on **three revenue streams**.

## 1. Organizer SaaS (Subscriptions)

Organizers pay for advanced management tools.

Typical premium features:

-   Custom branding
-   Advanced tournament formats
-   Unlimited participants
-   Advanced seeding
-   Tournament analytics
-   Export tools
-   Automation features

Typical pricing range:

-   \$5 -- \$20 / month

This is the **most stable revenue stream**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Entry Fee Commission

Organizers charge players to participate in tournaments.

Example:

Tournament entry: \$10\
Players: 32\
Prize pool: \$320

Platform takes:

5% fee = \$16

This model scales extremely well once large tournaments appear.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Event Management Tools

Professional organizers pay for tools such as:

-   Player check‑in systems
-   Scheduling tools
-   Live bracket updates
-   Broadcast overlays
-   Player verification

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Key Insight

The **customer who pays is the organizer**, not the player.

Players rarely pay for tournament tools.

Your monetization should focus on:

-   Tournament creators
-   Club organizers
-   Event managers

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Product Maturity Stages

Your product will likely evolve through the following stages:

Stage 1 --- Builder\
Stage 2 --- Early testers\
Stage 3 --- First real tournaments\
Stage 4 --- Community growth\
Stage 5 --- Monetization

Currently the project appears to be between **Stage 1 and Stage 2**.

The correct strategy is:

Adoption first → Monetization later.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Monetization Roadmap

## Phase 1 --- Early Product (Current Stage)

Goal:

Get organizers using the platform.

Monetization:

None.

But build **monetization hooks** into the architecture.

Key features:

-   Tournament ownership
-   Admin roles
-   Basic tournament limits
-   Tournament sharing

Example free plan:

-   2 tournaments
-   Basic features

Show:

"Premium features coming soon"

This validates demand without blocking adoption.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 2 --- Early Monetization

When you reach roughly:

20--50 active tournaments

Introduce **Organizer Pro**.

Example plan:

### Free

-   2 tournaments
-   16 players max
-   Basic bracket

### Organizer Pro (\$4.99/month)

-   Unlimited tournaments
-   Unlimited players
-   Advanced brackets

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 3 --- Organizer Tools

Once tournaments grow, introduce tools that save organizers time.

Possible features:

-   Advanced seeding
-   Match scheduling
-   Player check‑in
-   Bulk player import
-   Tournament templates

These features create **strong subscription value**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 4 --- Tournament Economy

Later the platform can support:

-   Entry fees
-   Prize pools
-   Platform commission

Example:

\$10 entry × 32 players = \$320 pool

Platform fee (5%) = \$16

This model can eventually outperform subscriptions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Feature Development Roadmap

## Step 1 --- Ownership Model

Add ownership and admin support to tournaments.

Fields:

-   ownerId
-   adminIds

Benefits:

-   Co‑organizers
-   Club management
-   Future permission expansion

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 2 --- Tournament Sharing

Each tournament should have a shareable link:

app.com/tournament/{id}

Players can:

-   View brackets
-   View matches
-   Share links

This creates natural viral growth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 3 --- Player Profiles

Each player should have:

-   Name
-   Avatar
-   Match history
-   Wins / losses

This creates **long‑term engagement**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 4 --- Tournament Templates

Allow organizers to reuse setups.

Example:

Weekly Club Tournament\
32 players\
Single elimination

Templates reduce organizer effort significantly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 5 --- Premium Features

Once usage grows, introduce premium tools such as:

-   Custom tournament logo
-   Custom colors
-   Remove platform watermark
-   Advanced formats

Branding features convert organizers very well.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Suggested Freemium Limits

### Free Plan

-   2 tournaments
-   16 players max
-   Single elimination format

### Premium Plan

-   Unlimited tournaments
-   Unlimited players
-   Advanced formats
-   Custom branding

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Upsell UX Strategy

Avoid aggressive blocking.

Instead of:

"You reached the limit."

Use:

"You have created 2 tournaments.

Upgrade to Organizer Pro to create unlimited tournaments and unlock
advanced features."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Long‑Term Growth Feature

A powerful future feature is **global player ranking**.

Example:

Players earn ranking points when they win tournaments.

Leaderboards:

-   Top players this month
-   All‑time rankings

This encourages players to remain active on the platform.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Strategic Advantage

As a small independent product you can focus on:

-   Simplicity
-   Mobile‑first design
-   Fast tournament creation
-   Beautiful UI
-   Speed

Large tournament platforms are often bloated and slow.

A focused product can compete effectively.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Priorities

Focus on these first:

1.  Make tournament creation extremely easy
2.  Make tournaments easy to share
3.  Give organizers clear ownership
4.  Grow real usage

Only after real adoption appears should monetization become aggressive.
